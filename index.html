What is Hydroponics?

Hydroponics is a method of growing plants without soil by using mineral nutrient solutions dissolved in water. Instead of soil, plant roots are supported by an inert medium (like rockwool, clay pebbles, or perlite) while the nutrient-rich water is circulated to provide all essential elements directly to the roots. Hydroponic systems allow precise control over nutrients, pH, temperature, and light, resulting in faster growth, higher yields, and lower water usage compared with traditional soil farming.
